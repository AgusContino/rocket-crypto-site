Not your keys, not your coins

"Not your keys, not your coins" is a phrase that is commonly used in the cryptocurrency community to emphasize the importance of self-custody of digital assets. It means that if you do not control the private keys to your cryptocurrency wallet, you do not truly own the coins or tokens that are stored in it.
Exchanges and wallets are two different types of platforms that are used to store and manage digital assets, specifically cryptocurrencies. When you store your cryptocurrency on an exchange, for example, you are trusting that the exchange will keep your funds safe and secure. But if the exchange gets hacked or goes out of business, you could lose your coins. By having control of your private keys, you can ensure that only you have access to your funds, and you are less exposed to the risk of hacking or fraud.
The phrase is a reminder for the user to be responsible for their own funds and not to trust third parties with the custody of their cryptocurrencies, because only the person who controls the private keys has full control over the funds.

Exchanges

An exchange is a platform that allows users to buy, sell, and trade cryptocurrencies with other users. Exchanges typically have a large selection of coins and tokens available for trading and they often also allow users to deposit and withdraw funds using traditional fiat currencies like the US dollar or Euro.

Wallets

A wallet is a platform that allows users to store and manage their digital assets in a safe and secure way. Wallets typically provide users with a unique set of private keys, which are used to access and manage the funds stored in the wallet. There are different types of wallets such as software wallets, which are installed on a user's computer or mobile device, and hardware wallets, which are physical devices that store the private keys.
